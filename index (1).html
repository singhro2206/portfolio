About

I spent seven years building large-scale data systems in banking and payments — high-volume PySpark pipelines, validation frameworks, the kind of work where a silent data error costs real money. That made me good at one thing in particular: finding where things quietly go wrong.

Now I'm moving that instinct into AI. Through an MSc in Data Science & AI at the University of Liverpool, my focus is mechanistic interpretability and RAG faithfulness — evaluating how language models reason, and whether they ground their answers in retrieved evidence or fall back on what they memorised. It's evaluation work at the level of the model's internals.

Dissertation · in progressRAG faithfulness

Does the model trust its memory or the document?

Using causal tracing on GPT-2 XL to resolve knowledge conflicts: feed the model a fact it knows, inject a contradicting retrieved document, and trace through the network to find where it decides to ground in the source vs. fall back on parametric memory. The evaluation criterion is faithfulness — and activation patching localises the layers where grounding succeeds or fails.

Python · PyTorch · activation patching · causal tracing · interpretability
